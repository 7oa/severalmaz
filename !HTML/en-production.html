Lomonosov diamond deposit

"Severalmaz" is engaged in the development of a diamond deposit named after MV. Lomonosov in Arkhangelsk region. The deposit consists of six kimberlite pipes: Arkhangelskaya, Karpinskogo-1, Karpinskogo-2, Pionerskaya, Pomorskaya, Lomonosovskaya, with a total resource more than 126 million tons of ore. Combination of mining and geological characteristics makes the deposit unique with no analogues in the world.

Severalmaz currently conducts open cast mining on two kimberlite pipes: Arkhangelskaya and Karpinskogo-1

Production cycle

Mining operations are conducted in the classical way, without the use of explosive drill, with the use of hydraulic excavators with bucket capacity from 7 to 15 m3.

Excavated ore and barren rock transported to ore storage areas and waste rock dumps respectively. Later, ore is transported to the processing plant, where it undergoes all stages of the process.

The processing technology, created by Russian specialists, is at the world standards level, and in some technological processes and equipment exceeds the world level.

This technology allows for maximum amount of diamonds extracted.

The technology stages are following:
  • Self-crushing in a mill, with the use of water, until the size of 25-30 mm.
  • Crushed rock is send to spiral classifiers, where it is separated, according to its density.
  • Further, the rock passes the sieving stage for 5 classes of size (fractions) with the use of screens of both domestic and imported production, later each fraction is processed separately.
  • Separation stage uses X-ray and dense medium separation methods, both effective for certain size class.
  • Final recovery stage extracts diamonds from separated concentrates. Diamonds are cleaned, sieved, and sent to diamond sorting shop, where they undergo a preliminary classification and sorting, with the selection of diamonds of rare color shades, and certification of diamonds of special sizes +10.8 carat.
